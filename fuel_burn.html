Fuel Burn Results

How to Interpret Outputs from Different EUROCONTROL Tools

Several EUROCONTROL aviation environmental tools provide fuel burn and CO₂-related outputs. These values are sometimes interpreted as directly comparable. In practice, differences in results are expected and arise mainly from differences in methodology, scope, system boundaries, level of aggregation and intended use of each tool/dashboard.

The table below provides a simplified overview of the typical scope and purpose of selected tools in order to support correct interpretation of results. Differences in outputs should be understood in this context.

Important interpretation note
Different tools quantify different aspects of fuel burn and emissions and are designed for different decision-making contexts (e.g. operational analysis, environmental reporting, scenario assessment, policy support). Differences in results therefore reflect differences in methodological design and intended use, and do not indicate inconsistency or relative accuracy of one tool compared to another. Results should not be directly compared without understanding the scope and assumptions of each tool.

 For detailed methodology, assumptions and calculation factors, see EUROCONTROL Standard Inputs for Economic Analyses:

  • Rate of fuel burn https://ansperformance.eu/economics/cba/standard-inputs/latest/chapters/rate_of_fuel_burn.html
  • Amount of emissions released by fuel burn https://ansperformance.eu/economics/cba/standard-inputs/latest/chapters/amount_of_emissions_released_by_fuel_burn.html
